Fluid Chef

Java Desktop Application. June 2020

The primary goal of this project was to create an application that met the needs of small catering and hospitality businesses who may not have sufficient infrastructure or resources to take advantage of a fully-fledged EPOS system. Initially a poster was designed to recieve critique on the topic.

Adequate analysis and planning procedures were implemented to ensure that the development process went smoothly. Meetings with a supervisor were held when possible, in order to gain feedback in terms of scope, documentation procedures and planning. Discussions were also held with several catering professionals in order to gain a better insight as to what features they would personally take advantage of, should they be included in Fluid Chef.

The development was a learning process that ultimately resulted in a successful application that met the requirements of the catering professionals in question, that also has the potential to be expanded in the future to improve performance and include a broader feature set.

Pok√©Quiz

Android Application/Game. April 2020.

This application aimed to be a gamified wiki, which allows users to answer continuous dynamically generated multiple-choice questions, all based around Pok√©mon from the Nintendo video game series. Users can both test their knowledge as well as gain new insight into various Pok√©mon from the series. Players earn score based on the number of questions they answer and the difficulty of those questions. Ultimately the application will be both educational and fun for the player.

The app also targeted a clean, intuitive and responsive graphical user interface in order to make using the app easy and instinctive for any user. Users will also be able to save their high scores on a local leader board and share their results across social network platforms such as Twitter.

Hercules

Unity Game. June 2019.

This project was undertaken as a group, Sam Buckingham, Jordan Honzik and I wanted to create a 2D platformer akin to similar games we had played in our childhoods. Inspiration was largely driven by titles such as Metroid and more modern takes on the genre such as Hollow Knight.

Hercules follows the story of a beetle who has had his girlfriend taken away from his home, in his adventure to get her back. Full story arc and game functiionality were determined before developing a slice of the game that would give players a taste of what the project could potentially offer.

Hercules Intro

Blender Modelling and Animation. June 2019.

During the development of Hercules, we also created a 30 second introduction animation to set the scene for the rest of the game. Sam Buckingham, Jordan Honzik and I worked collaboratively to story board and then 3D modelled a variety of assets for the final animation.

Our aim goal was to create a 3D yet low poly cutscene, that would compliment our 2D game, and would replicate the feel of a game from the 90's.

Lift Simulation

Java Desktop Application. January 2020.

The Lift Simulation application was created as part of a University module in Advanced Programming. The app was used to demonstrated an understanding of design patterns with a heavy influence from MVC and clean coding practices.

The simulation involved three test cases, each of which a different quantity of people would arrive at the lift on different floors, each class would act independantly of eachother.

A small amount of animation was included in the project in order to demonstrate for instance peoples movement and the opening and closing of doors.
